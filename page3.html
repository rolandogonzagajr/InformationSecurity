Common Race Condition Types

Limit Overrun Conditions

Limit overrun conditions, particularly in the context of race conditions, present a significant threat to the integrity and security of an application's business logic. This report delves into a specific scenario within an online store environment where the misuse of promotional codes exposes vulnerabilities. The application's susceptibility arises during the transition through a temporary sub-state, allowing users to exploit a race window and exceed imposed limits, potentially leading to misuse of discounts, gift cards, product ratings, and financial transactions.

Samples:

Promotional Code Exploitation:

  • Users exploit the race condition to use a promotional code multiple times.
  • Impact: Unintended discounts, potential revenue loss for the online store.

Exploiting Race Conditions in Gift Card Redemption:

  • Impact: Loss of value for the business, potential exploitation by users.

Users Manipulate the System to Submit Multiple Ratings for a Product:

  • Impact: Distorted overall product rating.

Users Attempt to Withdraw or Transfer Amounts Exceeding Their Account Balance:

  • Impact: Financial discrepancies and potential losses.

Multi-Endpoint Race Conditions

Multi-endpoint race conditions pose a significant threat, particularly when sending simultaneous requests to different endpoints. This report explores a common logic flaw in online stores where users can exploit the race condition by manipulating the order processing flow. Specifically, the vulnerability occurs when adding items to a cart, making a payment, and then adding more items before reaching the order confirmation page.

Sample:

  • Users can add more items during the critical window between payment validation and order confirmation.
  • Impact: Additional items may be included in the order before confirmation, leading to incorrect order states.
